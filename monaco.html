Introduction

Monaco is a small yet wealthy city-state on the French Riviera, known for its affluent population, luxury lifestyle, and tax advantages. With a small population, it has a unique blend of cultures and traditions.

Monaco is famous for hosting major events, including the Monaco Grand Prix, and is a hub for banking, tourism, and high-end real estate.

People and Society

Population

  • Total population: Just over 31,000
  • Population density: Among the highest globally
  • Main residential area: Capital, Monaco

Ethnic Groups

Group Percentage
Monegasque 32%
French 20%
Italian 15%
Other nationalities 33%

Languages

The official language of Monaco is French. Other widely spoken languages include:

  • English
  • Italian
  • Monegasque (the native language of the Monegasque people)

Religions

Monaco's main religion is Roman Catholicism, practiced by approximately 90% of the population. Other freely practiced religions include:

  • Protestantism
  • Islam
  • Judaism

Economy

Major Industries

  • Tourism
  • Real Estate
  • Insurance
  • Banking

Monaco’s tax haven status attracts wealthy individuals and businesses, significantly contributing to its economy.

Trade

Monaco’s primary trading partners are Italy, Switzerland, and Germany. Key export commodities include:

  • Jewelry
  • Perfumes
  • Watches
  • Packaged medicines
  • Cars

Currency & Banking

Monaco uses the euro (€) as its currency, despite not being part of the European Union. The banking sector is highly developed and plays a central role in the economy, with a focus on wealth management and private banking. International investors and businesses are drawn to Monaco for its favorable tax laws and financial stability.
