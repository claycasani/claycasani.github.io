Introduction

Monaco is a small but wealthy city-state located on the French Riviera, known for its affluent population, luxury lifestyle, and tax advantages. With a small population, it has a unique blend of cultures and traditions.

Monaco is famous for hosting major international events, including the Monaco Grand Prix, and is a major hub for banking, tourism, and high-end real estate.

People and Society

Population

Monaco has a population of just over 39,000 people, making it one of the smallest sovereign states in the world. The population density is one of the highest globally, with most residents living in the capital, Monaco.

Ethnic Groups

Monaco's population is a mix of different ethnicities, including French, Italian, and Monegasque. Approximately 28% of the population are native Monegasques, while the rest consists of French (47%) and Italians (15%), with the remainder made up of various other nationalities.

Religions

The official religion of Monaco is Roman Catholicism, practiced by approximately 90% of the population. However, other religions, including Protestantism, Islam, and Judaism, are freely practiced as well.

Economy

Major Industries

Monaco's economy is driven by tourism, real estate, and banking. Its status as a tax haven attracts wealthy individuals and businesses, contributing significantly to the nation's revenue.

Unemployment Rate

The unemployment rate in Monaco is exceptionally low, around 2%, owing to its affluent population and the number of employment opportunities within banking, tourism, and luxury services.
